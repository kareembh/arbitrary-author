What's up, Space Cowboys,

 

Today's Blogpost is brought to you by the **negative emotions and feelings** you have in your head. It's not going to be the most positive and acknowledgeable blog post. But it needs to be made.

 

This is a *public service announcement* to the **toxic positivity movement** that is happening throughout the world. To all that are part of that movement, this may be your cue to leave the site and continue scrolling on Instagram.

 

Simply pointing out that there is an issue which Yours Truly, *The Arbitrary Author*, needs to address.

 

On today's unscheduled public service announcement, we will be speaking on the topic of **Negative thinking**. I know what you all are thinking: this is going to get depressing real quick… I **THINK NOT!**

 

It might start a bit manic and depressive, but when you think of it, more *profound*. Without those depressive moments in life, you would never truly appreciate when things are going just perfectly. Almost like how you can never truly appreciate dusk without dawn?

 

Cowboys, I want to hear your voice. I want to know that I am not the only one that goes through these ups and downs on this journey that we call life. Or maybe it’s just my ADHD running amuck in my mind.

 

Now, let's get **serious** here, shall we?

\
**STORY TIME!!!**

 

It felt as if **everything was going wrong** in my third year of university, and I was **lost**. I was living in a house with four women, and being the only man, it was hard for me to feel like I fit in or that I wasn't **walking on eggshells to conform**. Seriously, they attacked for every nit-picking thing they could find.

 

Have you ever been blamed by your roommate for “going through their panties”? well I HAVE!!….words cannot describe the kind of insult I felt when my roommate blamed me for going through her panties when all I was doing was moving her shit from one machine to the other…

 

**I was bullied continuously and felt like my home was not mine**. I was confined to my bedroom when I had the time from my busy library schedule to find some relax time.

 

**I was depressed…**

 

I spent as much time out of the house and from people so I wouldn’t have to deal with  my uncomfortable reality. I would be asking for double shifts at work, followed by what seemed like an eternity living in the library. By living in the library, I mean that I was there so much that I should have paid rent at that point. I mean, it would probably be lower than the excruciatingly high price of student housing in Antigonish, Nova Scotia.

 

**I was stuck**, and I **was in a place where I had no direction**, and to top it all off in a relationship with someone I thought I loved, and I was going nowhere. What could I bring to the table? How could I make things flourish and grow when I could not. I was always told that parental sentence “you are going to be going places one day”. I was so lost that I started doubting myself worth and if I were even going to finish my year of University. I was stagnant and scared of what the future held for me.

 

I remember so vividly when my mom called me and as soon as I heard her voice I started crying into her ear. The sound pierced and resonated in her. She immediately knew there was something wrong. She voice was as if I was a child being given a pacifier to calm down after being woken. It was then that I realized how little care I put into my health, more specifically my mental health. If I were to keep going in this direction, it would be detrimental to my life.

 

Since that call, things have changed.

 

**Change is powerful and brings me to our second story**, which is much more current. We all remember the story of **Snow White**, don't we? You know, the ex I was so crazy for? Yeah her. Well, I recently found out that Snow White had moved on a lot faster than me in the break-up process.

 

Not only did she move on, but she wanted to meet up; she wanted to "**talk**" to "**catch up**" and not let things end "**negatively**" like she thought they did.

 

Well, I found out that she might have had ulterior motives for “catching up”. She then proceeds to tell me that her and my so-called "**friend**" slept together!! **RIGHT**!!? **Is that not all sorts of fucked up?**

 

One of the friends that I confided in, the friend I cried in front of, the one I told how much I was hurting and completely opened up, the one I told that I have never hurt like this in my life. Well, he went behind my back and fucked my ex-girlfriend. Which he says **"didn't mean to happen,**” anyone else believe him? Let me know I’ll be waiting.

 

Hearing about this felt like a knife slowly and thoughtfully placed into my heart and day by day, it went deeper and deeper into my being and my soul.

 

Well, I can tell you right now that I was going through a lot of negativity at the time. I wanted to reach out to the girl he was seeing and tell her what he had done. I wanted to make everyone hurt the way I ached at the moment.

 

The more and more I thought of it, the more it kept me up at night. I saw it while I slept. I thought of it during the day. It was like a waking nightmare that I thought only happened in the worst of movies.

 

**But it happened to me…**

 

I woke up one morning thinking that I needed a change. I needed to look at it differently. I realized in my meditation that this was not something to hold on to; I had to cut it out like cancer from my life to move forward.

 

I looked at this emotion inside myself and what it made me turn into, and I told myself that I would not hold hate in my heart for them. I showed love, and I wished nothing but the best to them moving on. Will I forget, no? I did not forgive and knowing myself. I may never forgive.

 

**But I will learn to love again.**

 

Looking back, I realize that even if they slept together out of ill-intent or by the heat of the moment crap, I came out of it this event stronger. I came out knowing which friends have my back and who is there for the sake of being there. I know who stands where.

 

I want to be honest about the concept of negative thinking. **We have this preconceived notion that life is linea**r. We move from being a child to a teenager to an adult and so on. That we experience only good as a child, then we experience love and life and all of the above, and it fits so well into place from the previous piece that we placed. **But it's an illusion.**

 

I can tell you right now from my experiences that life is far from linear. It is far from sunshine and rainbows, and it has love, hate, lust, envy, pride, gluttony, etc.

 

Preachers, News outlets, and your unkles best friends have told you; if you give in to the seven deadly sins, you are evil. Yet, life is not about running from them but playing a game of balance with them. It is about knowing when you should and shouldn't give in to those parts of your being.

 

You seek these external validations from people, places, foods, experiences, and so on. We get pleasure from some sins, and then a  cycle of guilting ourselves about how bad it was begins again.

 

But life is about appreciating the ups AND the downs. Because without one, you will not have the other. I only started to realize this theory since my mindfulness practice. You know that without hate, there is no love that you can apply to anything in life.

 

We need to start appreciating the work that it takes to be happy, the joy in going through the mud and coming out a different person. The hard work that it takes to get back up time after time of failure.

 

But we need to remember that this is my thought process on the matter. This mindset is how I work things out in my head to rhyme to the reason of life. I tell myself this because I go through the mud on a regular. I tell myself that without one, you cannot have the other. It is like the eternal yin and yang that goes on and on.

 

I want to know your thoughts on negative emotions and how you manage to work with them in everyday life.

 

Because negativity will creep in, no matter if you are the happiest person in the world or not, negative emotions will come up. But with what I have realized over my practice, it's not about not having those feelings. It's about finding love in the negative, and it's about the peace you see in all the emotions that humans have.

 

And on that note, see you, I'll SEE YOU, SPACE COWBOYS….
