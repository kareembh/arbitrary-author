Good Morning, Cowboys

 

Deciding what to write about this week has been more challenging. I feel as things begin to ramp up and changes happen, especially this week, that getting my foot in the right direction was stopping me from doing just that. 

 

With that said, I wanted to give you guys an update on all the good, the bad, and the totally arbitrary life of The Arbitrary Author.

 

As many of you already know, I have been conducting Coffee-Break-style interviews. For those of you waiting with your Double Double cup of coffee from Tims (For all my fellow Canadians out there), put that shit away because you're here for me to rant about my new talk show: *Coffee Break.*

 

I honestly had no idea that I would be where I am after going through such a mind fuck that was last year… guys; seriously… this was a whole cluster fuck of a year. Think about Godzilla crushing his way through Japan, oh and I was Japan in this beautifully painted mental picture! 

 

So this whole Coffee Break thing started like anything else in my life, arbitrarily, which is honestly the only way things should begin. It started when I was still having people reaching out and wanting me to guest on their podcasts! People keep repeatedly saying about the massive potential of starting my own show. I kept hearing that I had the PERFECT personality for this type of thing. 

 

Well, fast forward a bit, and yes, I did start a show (and personally, I think it's incredible, but maybe that's just the way it makes me feel), so thank you, everyone, for pushing me forward. But back up, I was so scared. I was scared that people wouldn't want to hear what I have to say or what type of content I would be pushing out. Which, in all honesty… fuck it. 

 

So there I was, all fermuffled. Thinking this is a good idea, thinking of all the ways I could push value forward in the form of content. I usually am the brainstorming friend of the group, but when it came to my own thoughts and ideas, it's like I hit a metaphorical brick wall, and I'm just staring at the wall, looking like an idiot. 

 

It was at that exact moment that I finally got my million-dollar idea. I mean less so of a million-dollar idea and more so the effect of reaching the goal of 1million people reached by Coffee-Break. There I was with Rocky, a tattoo artist I've been visiting for some time, the cat clawing at my fresh. Artist pushing tattoo magic! (thanks again Rocky, you can explain to my tattoo artist why his beautiful work is all jacked up)

 

Sorry, where was I again? AH yes, I remember now. That was ADHD at it's finest. 

 

So I finally had my million-dollar idea; I wanted to contribute to the ADHD community. I am so thankful for the community for being there for me when I was at my lowest that it was my turn to give back and make sure that I was giving and helping contribute the best I could to them when they had been there for me.

 

When I say, I felt like I never felt like I fit in. I mean it. You know, constantly feeling like I was the first of the friends in the group to get the boot. Well, that was me till I found the community. I felt like my outlook on life changed. I felt like I was finally part of a tribe, that people understood me, which I was no longer aimlessly wondering. I was found. 

 

With that being said, we are at the point where I am trying to understand what I can do to make sure that I am giving back rather than consistently taking my fat kid's piece of the cake. So there I am, spending days trying to understand, and when it came to me like that thought you have in the shower but can't do shit about it unless you run naked around the house to find a pen and paper… (But maybe that's just me)

 

I will be trying to get into the niche of ADHD. Because we are A) fucking amazing (pardon my French) B) The community is ABSOLUTELY HUGE!!!

 

My first step was to ensure I was not going to make this podcast too lenghtly because I am trying to appeal to the ADHD mind, which is like trying to tame a squirrel. The first thing was making the episodes no more than 20 minutes (Which was a lot harder than I thought. Thank you @bermudaCal for helping a brother out)

 

Secondly, I needed to find the right type of audience to bring the most amount of value. Now, how the fuck am I supposed to do that? I started small, and I mean tiny. I started moving my way up once I started getting some traction, and I know we're starting to reach places, finally surpassing my goal of 1M people. 

 

To no surprise, the first episode lasted 45 minutes. Well, I more than doubled the time I wanted to make it. Well, at least it was something that I could pitch to the potential future interviewee. Because who wants to chat with a random guy for 30 minutes about their ADHD experience?

 

Now to give you some perspective on everything since we started recording Coffee-Break. We have finally come to the end of the first season of Coffee Break. All interviews are done. I have put together more than 11 episodes. Making it longer than any Netflix original. So you are in for a treat, guys!

 

We are not (do you mean now? It just feels like you should if you haven't) starting to reach out to sponsors, Not For Profit organizations (in which we have been accepted), and all interviewees are on board! So let's get it! 

 

I am starting to see things come together, and my dreams of getting into the community have become a reality more so than just some dream that I was hoping to reach. 

 

I just wanted to thank you, the reader, the listener, the wonderers, and over all the people with no place. Because now and forever, you will always be welcome to the land of misfit toys. 

 

With that said, this is the end of the long blog post, and as always:

 

See you, Space Cowboys …
