ADHD & Friendship - the good, the bad, and HYPER-ACTIVE.

 

Greetings from the Arbitrary Author, 

 

In today's little blog post, I will be speaking to you about that "group". You know the one with the people? The ride or dies? The supportive? And the outright annoying. 

 

On this week's blog, we will be going into detail about….if you haven't already guessed…

 

FRIEND GROUPS and honestly just friends in general. More specifically, I will be talking about how to deal with Friendship as an ADHD'er. My Do's and Don'ts of my friend groups and how I learnt to deal with everything in-between. 

 

Like many of you here reading this right now, I find that people within the ADHD community are original and authentic people! We make friends quickly, we are outspoken, loud, obnoxious, all-around fun, and we are playful…at times…

 

On the other side, we can be forgetful, impulsive (TOTALLY ME), distracted, indifferent, socially awkward (at times), disorganized. The list could go on for hours…so if I'm missing something. Let me know?...

 

Now, let's get serious. How many of you have forgotten a birthday? A plan that you made? How your hyper fixation got in the way of your friends and thus made you feel like you were the BAD friend. But in reality, you were the one that needed those plans in the first place, and it just slipped. 

 

Just remember, you're not alone. Shit happens…I mean, it happens to me all the time if we're going to be completely honest. 

 

Sometimes, being ADHD doesn't feel like a superpower and more like a weight on you. Just remember, THAT IS OKAY!

 

Having ADHD can really complicate your life, socially in specific for me at least. If what I'm saying is on point. LET ME KNOW. I want to hear your story!

 

Back to the story at hand…my ADHD got me a little sidetracked. It's like going in circles and somehow taking the wrong turn every time, I swear to god! 

 

**HOW ADHD MAKES RELATIONSHIPS HARD…**

![](/assets/hard-times.png)

 

We can start by all the dates that we forget to set in our daily schedule, or even by all the times that we forgot to do something that you told your roommate you would do. 

 

***STORYTIME!!***

 

I swear, I'm still in awe that my roommate and I are celebrating our 1st anniversary of living together; that's 5 / 6 years we've been the best of friends! That is a MILESTONE in my life.

 

I have been gifted with roommates that get sick of my constant forgetfulness, my "clutter," as I like to call it and my manic behaviour. Oh, and I can be an overall space cadet at times.

 

**BUT THIS GUY IS A KEEPER**. Love him like a brother.

 

With that being said, I have a problem with not washing my dishes very well, specifically, the cutlery. I swear it's like I have a personal issue with washing my cutlery. The fun part of this all is that for the last year, I SWEAR TO GOD I have heard my roommate tells me at least 100 times that I need to start cleaning my forks and knives…do I do it?...hell no. But the fact that he keeps reminding me is what makes it all better. 

 

I can successfully tell you now that I STILL DON'T clean them. But I try a lot harder (let's hope he doesn't read this post…)

 

So when it comes to friends and how having ADHD makes it hard to maintain those friendships. You need to accept the fact that you are who you are. Nothing will change that, but keep trying, keep looking if need be. Because it is these parts of our personalities that make some people really LOVE us. Don't settle for people that don't care about us; we are the givers, the hard workers, the absolutely forgetful. But you know what? In reality? We are awesome!

 

I think that we need to realize that being forgetful, being impulsive, distracted. All the other "issues" that come with ADHD make it a bit harder for us to KEEP friends (because let's be real here, we make them hella easy..) So, I will explain further in this post. We may need to adapt. Like we do so well!

 

I know that my distractibility, emotional irregularity, manic behaviours, and just overall what makes me "ME" are the fact that I own up to them. I know that I am an all-or-nothing kind of guy. People will either LOVE or HATE me. I am okay with that now. 

 

In my late teens and early 20's, I thought that I would need to be friends with everyone because I had issues in myself that I needed to get sorted out. As I grow older, the more I realize that it's not about how many friends you have but the quality of friends you have. 

 

I know for a fact that I can depend on my friends for LIFE. They will support me through anything, and everything and I can say that I am BLESSED to have them. To help, support, nurture and push me to be the person I need to be. 

 

 

**WHAT I DID TO HELP WITH KEEPING MY FRIENDS**

 

![](/assets/snoopy.png)

 

What I feel like I should point out is that this is a work in progress. I am nowhere close to perfect in any sense of the way. Nor do I want to be, honestly. I think that my personality makes me the loving person that many of my friends think of me (or at least I hope…)

 

But I think that we can name a couple things that I am being conscious about to make sure that I am being the best friend that I can personally be. 

 

First of which being: **Actively Listen.** 

Wait for your friend to speak and absorb everything that they are saying, rather than just waiting for them to stop talking so that you can add what you have had in your head for the last 5 minutes of your friends speaking to you. 

 

What this does for me: this really helps me understand my friend's point of view, more so than anything. It also makes me follow conversations a lot quicker and better overall. 

 

Another thing that I am actively using in my day-to-day life is **Think Ahead.** 

 

I could not be where I am today if it was not for my meticulous lists and schedules. I find that my biggest issue was completely forgetting that I had plans in the first place. I find that if it's in my schedule (where I look every day for my upcoming events), I will never forget a friend or a plan that I had made previously. 

 

This could help so many people; I didn't even realize or even start using this as a tool until I was in university! It has helped me SIGNIFICANTLY! So keep it in mind if you have that issue as well. 

 

Finally, **SEEK HELP!**

 

This, more than all of the tools and tricks I have implemented in my life, is the most important!

 

The reason I say this isn't because I think everyone should go see a therapist. But it is the fact that it helps you realize where your head is at. Therapy gives you an unbiased opinion of what your choices do to others and an honest opinion of what you put out rather than just your friends and family telling you. 

 

I can honestly say that therapy FOR ME has been the most valuable part of my whole transition to being the person I am meant to be. 

 

It helped me realize that I didn't actively listen, nor did I wait for others to stop speaking, and how much my actions affected the others around me. 

 

But look, I think that everyone has their own way of approaching these issues that many of us with ADHD or even parents with children with ADHD have to face. It's not a race to see how to do it fastest. It's a journey and a process to implement into your everyday life. 

 

Like I previously have mentioned, I am nowhere close to being perfect. I would love some ideas that you use. 

 

I know that I am not alone, so I want to hear your voice and what tools you have used to grow. As well, if you have any stories, you wish to voice! 

 

Until then, ***SEE YOU, SPACE COWBOY……***
