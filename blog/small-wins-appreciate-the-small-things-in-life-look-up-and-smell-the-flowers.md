Good Afternoon Space Cowboys,

 

In lieu of my recently passing dog, I thought it would be best to chat about something which I started appreciating more and more as of late. This week's topic at hand will be about *enjoying the small wins in life*. Like my mom always told me, **you need to smell the roses in life.**

 

We spend too much of our time consistently trying to reach a goal. I'll be the first to admit it. I am the type of person to completely lose sight of the smaller things in life to achieve my long term goals. Which looking back now, means I’m basically a space cadet for 50% of my life!

 

I was/am the type of person that basically wasted a significant portion of my life trying to reach a particular milestone. When that milestone was reached, it was straight on to the next one. I was never focusing on how the present could make me appreciate the end goal even more.

 

When I was younger, I always felt when my parents gave me the good old "slow down and smell the roses," I would just repeat in my head, "blah blah blah," going in one ear and quickly spilling out the other. I never really had a proper understanding of what it meant to indeed be in the present moment. I mean, I don't blame 13 year old me for not really looking into it. I was way into Pokémon and Dragon Ball Z at that point to realize anything outside of the 20" TV we had in our family living room.

 

It is only as of recently that I am starting to enjoy the slower things. I think that there have been two pivotal moments in my life that have helped me realize more than ever the concept of enjoying and being fully in the moment.



**STORYTIME:**

 

I remember precisely one day during my Christmas break in Montreal after finishing finals during my third year of university. I tried to recalibrate my mind, body, and soul after being completely mind fucked by finals. My mom barges into my room and tells me that we will be going for a walk; my parents had just moved into a new apartment in Westmount and wanted to show me around the new part of Montreal. Unwilling to go because of my major withdrawals from my medication, I accept the offer.

As we were walking around Westmount, I was consistently staring down at my phone, not taking the time to participate in conversation.

 

At that EXACT moment of my blissful ignorance, I ran into a pole and dropped and cracked my phone. As I look up, hating the world and everything around it. I noticed my mom hysterically laughing at me; not only did I not listen to my mom before hitting the pole, BUT SHE EVEN WARNED ME ABOUT MY INPEDING CONCUSION!!

 

I look up, dazed and confused by everything that had just happened. Looked at my mom, laughed, gave her the biggest hug. Looked her in the eye and told her, "That I would try."

 

I would try to be more present, I would try to work on myself, I would try to appreciate everything in the now.

 

She then followed up by giving me advice that I have yet to forget. She saw how tired I was, how overworked I was, how much my goals of finishing university, getting a job, starting a family, etc were taking me away from how much my inner dialogue would change if I just started to love the journey rather than just the destination.

 

I have always had the most troubles with being in the present. I am going to be completely honest. Even at this EXACT moment, I am thinking of all the plans I have to put together to reach my 30-year goal.

 

But, the one thing that I have learnt is that it is not about consistently creating goals. Because, more than anything, I think that creating goals, ESPECIALLY for the neurodiverse community, is positive. It is about being present when creating those goals. It is about appreciating the road that you take to achieving those goals.

 

In my mind, it is all about polarity, a give and take, a cosmic touch and go. It is about loving everything you do and doing what you love but being mindful of its reasoning.

 

Let's be honest here. I will screw up. I will have weeks where I am more present than others.

 

The only person that I am competition within my life is The Arbitrary Author.

 

Now let me hear from you; if you have made it this far, let me know what you think. What are some little victories, some of the small things that you have picked up on as of recently! I will be waiting to hear your voice!

 

So with that, I'LL SEE YOU, SPACE COWBOY
